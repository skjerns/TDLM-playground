What am I looking at?

The left panel shows the simulated classifier probabilities — one trace per state. A replay event is a sequence of Gaussian "reactivation" bumps, each lagged by the chosen time from the previous state (dotted lines mark event onsets).

The right panel shows TDLM sequenceness vs. time lag. Forward measures evidence for replay in the sequence order, backward the reverse, and difference their contrast. Dashed/dash-dot lines are permutation significance thresholds; with rescaling on, the permutation-max threshold sits at ±1, so a curve poking above ±1 is significant at p<.05. A clean forward replay shows a forward peak at the lag you set between states.

Turn on alpha correction to add time-shifted confound regressors (as in Liu et al., 2021) that suppress spurious sequenceness from oscillations at the given frequency.
